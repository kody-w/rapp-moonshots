# Shark Tank verdict

## Winner — Ghost Ops

**Ghost Ops wins Moonshot 002.**

It creates the clearest new application category: a flight simulator for
AI-native operations where infrastructure becomes conversational, specialists
disagree in public, operator choices change the incident, and every rehearsal
produces a recovery playbook.

Measured evidence:

- 12 deterministic engine/export tests;
- 20 structural and safety checks;
- 200 matched policy experiments;
- containment-first won 200/200 comparisons;
- mean containment score improved by 42.995 points; and
- simulated spread fell from 4.585 to 0.625 events per run.

The honest boundary: fixture incidents are not proof of production incident
outcomes. They prove the application engine, interaction, deterministic replay,
and experiment—not real-world security effectiveness.

## Category awards

### Strongest immediate developer value — Counterfactual Repro Lab

It transforms “works on my machine” debugging from log comparison into bounded
causal experiments.

- 27 tests;
- 36 isolated trials;
- 9/9 reproducible baseline failures;
- 18/18 rejected-control failures;
- 9/9 repeatable causal passes;
- 100% seeded cause accuracy; and
- 36/36 verified workspace cleanups.

### Best trust and recovery proof — Resurrection Proof

It changes backup assurance from “files exist” to “the recovered behavior works
and rejects corruption.”

- 14 tests;
- 10/10 clean restores;
- 40/40 behavioral canaries;
- 10/10 injected corruptions hard-failed; and
- 10/10 isolated workspaces cleaned.

## Why these are moonshots

Each prototype starts with a narrow, working application and points toward a
category-scale future:

- **Resurrection Proof:** universal recovery contracts for autonomous systems.
- **Counterfactual Repro Lab:** programmable causal debugging across real environments.
- **Ghost Ops:** continuously rehearsed, self-documenting operational resilience.

The prototypes do not claim those futures already exist. They demonstrate a
credible wedge, measurable evidence, and an interaction people can experience
today.
