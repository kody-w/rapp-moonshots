# Experiment

## Hypothesis

A persistent center plus cumulative spatial shells will make a long voice-first
sequence recoverable from memory, while separating preview from a spoken
**choose** boundary prevents webcam noise from committing actions.

The falsifiable claim is not that a webcam can track eyes or hands. It is that
six large directions, explicit confidence gates, and reversible depth are
enough to complete the fixed tournament task without pointer or keyboard after
one permission action.

## Protocol

1. Serve `index.html` on localhost and grant camera/microphone once.
2. Begin with head and hands neutral for 480 ms.
3. Complete the published cobalt-beacon task.
4. At schedule depth, enter an incorrect time and say “undo.”
5. Interrupt the camera after a preview, attempt to choose, reconnect it, and
   continue.
6. Export metrics and replay JSON.
7. Verify the final route, zero media persistence, freeze behavior, and
   recovery behavior independently from the UI.

The deterministic condition at `?simulate=1` fixes every action timestamp and
is the reproducible acceptance fixture. A human trial must report hardware,
browser, recognition implementation, lighting, and whether `FaceDetector`
existed; those variables are not hidden inside the deterministic result.

## Instrumentation

The state machine records semantic events only: depth, option ID, source,
bounded confidence, timestamp, block reason, commit, undo, sensor loss, and
recovery. It does not retain pixels, grayscale arrays, face boxes, audio, or
transcript audio. Exported metrics include task exactness, timing, commits,
false commits, blocked commits, undo, dwell cancellation, voice repair,
accepted/rejected gestures, fallback use, sensor loss, and recovery time.

The ninth commit is the intentional 15:00 tunnel. Undo removes it before the
14:30 commit, so the final route exactly equals the eight expected option IDs.
The frozen-period intent is rejected and cannot become a pending preview.

## Acceptance gates

- deterministic output is byte-stable in meaning across repeated runs;
- all eight final option IDs match the published task;
- preview, motion enter, low-confidence voice, short dwell, cooldown, and
  frozen input cannot commit;
- all motion coordinates/confidences remain bounded;
- camera loss preserves committed layers and cancels pending input;
- replay sequence numbers are contiguous and prove final exact state;
- built HTML has no external asset, network, recording, or persistence API;
- both Clawpilot themes and the explicit access fallback are present.

## What remains to measure with people

Completion speed, recognition repairs, fatigue, accidental back gestures,
lighting sensitivity, learnability after a day, and parity of switch access
must be measured in consented trials. The deterministic fixture proves logic
and recovery, not usability or universal accessibility.
