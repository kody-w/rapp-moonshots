# Adaptive Orb pitch

Adaptive Orb is an eyes-up operating layer for AI in kitchens, workshops,
field work, accessibility, and anywhere hands or attention are occupied. AI
conversation inherited a text-message rectangle; Adaptive Orb gives each
thought the interaction shape it needs.

One conversation changes grammar without changing memory:

- Voice Orbit catches a broad walking note or hands-busy intent.
- Gaze Compass stabilizes a short field checklist or bounded decision.
- Gesture Tunnel carries a kitchen guide, revision, or deeper scenario and
  gets back to the exact parent.

The mobile-first center remains readable at arm’s length and always means rest.
A phone shows four primary choices before refinement. `repeat`, `stop`, `undo`,
and `what changed` are global voice and large fallback actions. Gaze never
means consent; an explicit voice, gesture, touch, keyboard, or switch action
confirms. Captions mirror earcons, and haptics are supported-only and opt-in.

Value arrives before permission: start with the complete sensor-free AI, then
optionally add microphone and front camera one at a time. Backgrounding stops
live sensors and resumes sensor-free without losing conversation state. The
installable offline iOS shell remains useful even when standalone hardware or
speech APIs degrade; it never equates installation with live-sensor support.

The public PWA needs no account, key, dependency, or AI network. Its
deterministic responder makes the whole experience inspectable on GitHub Pages
and offline. Teams already running RAPP Brainstem can add one stdlib
same-origin companion; secrets stay server-side, and failure returns visibly
to demo AI without discarding the conversation.

The 12-turn replay proves all scenarios and grammars, undoes a wrong branch,
then completes the exact cobalt-beacon safety task. It exports semantics, not
words. Fingerprint `071ba015` verifies the conversation; `c1b6e39f` remains the
unchanged task-safety baseline. Separate mobile evidence proves the 390×844 and
844×390 contracts, progressive permissions, four-choice limit, and
interruption recovery.

Adaptive Orb does not claim perfect AI, eye tracking, universal PWA sensing, or
safe distracted use. It is not for driving or safety-critical control. It
makes a narrower claim: AI can stay useful while hands are busy and attention
is shared—without losing memory, agency, privacy boundaries, or safety state.
