# Adaptive Orb pitch

AI conversation inherited a rectangle from text messaging. Adaptive Orb asks a
better question: what interaction shape fits the thought happening now?

One conversation changes grammar without changing memory:

- Orbit catches the ambiguity of “help me create.”
- Compass resolves the precision of “which priority?”
- Tunnel carries the depth of “explain this layer, revise it, and get me back.”

There is no chat box, modal handoff, iframe, or per-mode transcript. The
persistent center always means rest. Gaze never means consent. Stop, cancel,
undo, stale sensing, and old AI responses fail closed.

The public PWA needs no account, key, dependency, or AI network. Its
deterministic responder makes the complete experience inspectable on GitHub
Pages and offline. Teams that already run RAPP Brainstem can add one stdlib
same-origin companion; secrets remain server-side and failure returns visibly
to demo AI without discarding the conversation.

The 12-turn replay proves Create, Plan, Explain, and Navigate across all three
grammars, undoes a wrong conversational branch, then completes the exact
cobalt-beacon safety task. It exports semantics, not words. Fingerprint
`071ba015` verifies the conversation; `c1b6e39f` remains the unchanged
task-safety baseline.

Adaptive Orb does not claim perfect AI, gaze, privacy, or universal access. It
makes a narrower and more useful claim: an AI conversation can adapt its
interaction shape while keeping memory, agency, privacy boundaries, and safety
state continuous.
